People often ask whether online or in-person counselling would suit them better. There is no single answer. What matters is what feels private, realistic, and workable in your life.

## In-person sessions

In-person counselling can suit people who:

- prefer a clear separation from home life
- find it easier to focus in a dedicated room
- want the steadiness of travelling to an appointment

Marie offers in-person sessions in Kanturk, Co. Cork.

## Online sessions

Online support may be worth asking about if:

- travel is difficult
- work or family demands make attendance harder
- privacy at home can still be arranged

The site does not assume online appointments are always available, so it is best to ask directly if this format would suit you better.

## Questions that can help you decide

- Where would I feel more private?
- Which option is more realistic week to week?
- Would travelling help me prepare, or add strain?
- Do I have a quiet space if I am online?

## It does not have to be perfect

The best choice is often the one that makes it easier to begin and easier to continue. If you are unsure, a short conversation on the [Contact page](/contact) can help clarify what may suit you.
