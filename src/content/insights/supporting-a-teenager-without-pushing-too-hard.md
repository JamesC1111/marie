Adults often want to help quickly when a young person seems withdrawn, tense, or under pressure. The difficulty is that worry can easily turn into pressure.

What may help:

- keeping the tone steady rather than urgent
- asking simple questions instead of long ones
- offering options rather than demands
- recognising that timing matters

If youth support may be needed, [Jigsaw Cork](/resources) is one of the official services listed on the site, and Marie's [support for children and young people](/services/support-for-children-and-young-people) page explains the practice side carefully.
